Void Hunter

Ever wanted to be invisible for the rest of your life? Run this class!

With Omnioculus and/or Gyrfalcon's Hauberk, this class will make you never seen again!

*This build is mostly for late/end game PVE content (GM Nightfalls, Legendary dungeons, raids, etc.).

**Really not good for PVP content.

Weapons

A few weapons that will really make you invisible!

    Kinetic Slot
  • Run some champion stunner (Wish-ender, Arbalest, etc. for exotics).
  • Any non-exotic stunner will work, probably.
    • Energy Slot
  • If no exotic in Kinetic, this is where the fun begins.
  • Wavesplitter is wonderful for applying suppression and getting some juicy resets.
  • Lemon Arque is great for AOE and staying the hell away from the big boys.
  • If exotic in Kinetic, just run some void stunner.
  • Legendary Void trace rifle exists, but it kinda sucks.
    • Heavy Slot
  • Any void hard hitting heavy weapon (LFR, Rockets, maybe even a HEAVY MACHINE GUN).
  • Currently running Braytech Osprey with some auto reload (Autoloading and envious assassin are my main two).

Armor

  • Gyrfalcon's Hauberk for gigachad volatile AOE. Could even get a nice overshield and damage boost if you finish an enemy while invisible.
  • Omnioculus for very safe and LOTS OF invisible saves. Teammates will really like you.
  • For armor slots, focus on melee and class ability reduction. Grenade is whatever.
  • Always make sure to grab elemental resistance on chest. Grandmaster Nightfalls are no joke.

Aspects and Fragments

  • Stylish Executioner
  • Focus on going invisible over and over again from ad clearing.
  • More gigachad playstyle, but not as consistent since you need to kill things for invisible.
  • Pair with Gyrfalcon's Hauberk for very long invisibility times.
  • Vanishing Step
  • Very consistent invisibility.
  • Pair with Stylish Executioner for easy Gyrfalcon's Hauberk resets.
  • Pair with Trapper's Ambush for more team invisibility.
  • Trapper's Ambush
  • The bread and butter of Omnioculus.
  • Make you and your teammates tankier in general.
  • Revive and invis your teammates for safe consistent gameplay.
  • Remember, the damage resistance only happens while invisible, so the moment you or your teammates shoot, they are back to squishy.
  • If you really don't want to play aggressive, just play back with a long-range weapon and revive your teammates.

Playstyle

  • Focus on ad clearing, stay pretty far back from most of the action.
  • You can be aggressive, but the moment your resources are on cooldown, you're a sitting duck for oneshots.
  • Tip: When reviving teammates, jump and use Trapper's Ambush at ~80% full. You'll invis yourself and your teammate while also giving the damage resistance for much safer repositioning.
  • Omnioculus allows for a lot of restealth if you stealth 2 or more teammates at a time.
  • You cannot restealth teammates if they are already invisible, meaning your melee won't come back as fast.
